Participation and Researcher Opportunities

Most of CRF's fieldwork takes place in caves on state or federal lands. While extensive caving expertise isn’t required, participants should have basic caving skills, some experience, and their own gear with a working knowledge of its use.

To apply for CRF membership, you must provide a brief summary of your caving experience and explain your interest in CRF. If you have no underground experience, we recommend connecting with a local caving club. The National Speleological Society website lists caving organizations by state.

CRF activities differ from sport caving. Due to limited field support needs and space at field stations, new personnel are accepted at a controlled rate.

Membership Information

CRF operates in several regions across the U.S. If you're interested in joining, please select a primary operations area from the list below. Once accepted, you’re welcome to participate in any operations area.

CRF does not share your personal information with any other party or organization. Your information will not be retained or used for any purpose other than to respond to this inquiry.

How to Apply

  • For Ozark Operations: Email your caving experience and interests to the Ozark Operations Personnel Officer at Ozarks@Cave-Research.org.
  • For all other areas: Send an email to Membership@Cave-Research.org with answers to the following questions:
  1. Name:
  2. Email Address:
  3. Briefly summarize your caving experience:
  4. Why are you interested in CRF?
  5. Which operations area are you interested in?
    (Select only one primary area. See our Operations Page for areas of operation)
