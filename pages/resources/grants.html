The Philip M. Smith Graduate Research Grant for Cave and Karst Research

Deadline: March 1, 2025

In 2015, the Cave Research Foundation (CRF) named its graduate research grant program after Philip M. Smith, CRF’s founding president (1957-1965). A Springfield, Ohio native and Ohio State University graduate in geology and science education, Smith (1932-2014) was a leader in science, technology, and public policy for over 50 years. He contributed to polar research programs with the National Academy of Sciences, National Research Council, and National Science Foundation and advised Presidents Nixon, Ford, and Carter on science policy. In the 1950s-60s, Smith was active in caving, co-founding the Central Ohio Grotto of the National Speleological Society and joining the NSS C3 expedition in Floyd Collins Crystal Cave, Kentucky. At a time when few American scientists studied caves, the C3 expedition highlighted the need for sustained exploration. CRF was founded to support such research, inspired partly by Smith’s work on the International Geophysical Year. From its start, CRF has prioritized multidisciplinary research. Honoring Smith’s legacy, CRF offers grants for master's and doctoral research in cave and karst studies across earth, natural, and social sciences. The CRF Board of Directors sets the funding cap annually, typically awarding four to six grants of $1,000 to $3,000.

Application Guidelines

Deadline: March 1 (annually) Submission: Email a single PDF (preferred) or DOCX/RTF file to Dr. Patricia Kambesis (pat.kambesis@wku.edu) and Dr. Kayla Sapkota (knsapkota@asub.edu). Paper copies are not accepted. Applicants must be enrolled in a degree-granting institution, with preference for thesis or dissertation projects. U.S. and international students may apply, but submissions must be in English.

    Required Materials:
  • Title & Abstract – A title page with a ≤250-word abstract for a non-specialist audience. Include your department, major advisor, and degree level (master’s or doctoral).
  • Research Proposal – A ≤10-page, single-spaced proposal (12 pt. font, 1-inch margins), including tables/figures (references excluded from the limit).
    Cover the research problem, background, significance (especially to cave and karst studies), and methods.
  • Budget & Schedule – Detail research funding needs and indicate other funding sources or grant applications.
  • Curriculum Vitae (CV) – List publications, conference presentations, honors, and relevant research qualifications.
  • Letters of Reference – Two letters, including one from your graduate advisor or committee chair. Referees may send letters directly to the grant program chairs.
  • Review Process: Proposals are evaluated by a panel of karst scientists (e.g., geologists, biologists, hydrologists, archaeologists). Research with broad significance to cave and karst studies is more likely to receive funding.
  • Pre-Submission Inquiry: Applicants are encouraged to contact the grant committee chairs before submitting a full proposal to discuss project suitability.

Grant Conditions

  • University Account: If possible, set up a grant fund account through your university. Check with your research and grants office before applying.
  • Funding Restrictions: University overhead (Facilities & Administrative Costs) cannot be charged to the grant.
  • Project Timeline: The grant period starts on the award letter date and lasts one year. If additional time is needed, request a no-cost extension with justification and an estimated completion date.
  • Disbursement: The full award amount will be sent to the institution at the start of the grant. Provide remittance details to the grant program chair and CRF Treasurer.
  • Financial Report: Submit a brief expenditure report at the project’s end. Any unspent funds must be returned to CRF.
  • Final Abstract: Within three months of project completion, submit an extended abstract (≤1,000 words, with up to three figures/tables and a permalink to the full paper) for the CRF Annual Report.
  • Acknowledgment: Credit CRF as a research supporter in all related publications.

Grant Budget

Allowable Budget Items

  • Travel Costs: Includes transportation to and from the field or within the field. If driving, itemize costs based on university motor pool rates or reasonable personal vehicle rates.
  • Per Diem: Daily allowances for food and lodging while in the field. Costs should be itemized and reasonable.
  • Expendable Supplies: Field or laboratory supplies necessary for the project. Itemize expenses.
  • Specialized Analyses: Costs for essential analyses such as radiometric dating, isotopic studies, or specimen thin sections.
  • Specialized Equipment: Justifiable expenses for necessary field or laboratory equipment, such as dedicated data loggers, cave gear, or other specialized measuring instruments.

Unallowable Budget Items

  • Facility and Administrative Costs: These expenses are not covered by the grant.
  • Salary: Compensation for principal investigators or student employees is not allowed. However, salary for field workers in the host country may be permitted if justified.
  • General Equipment: Long-term use items such as computers, cameras, scales, and software are typically not allowed unless justified under specialized equipment needs.
  • Conference Expenses: Travel, lodging, and registration fees for scientific conferences are not covered.
