Research · Preserve · Protect

The Cave Research Foundation is a private, non-profit organization dedicated to advancing the study, management, and interpretation of caves and karst. Through partnerships and exploration, we work to ensure the long-term conservation of these vital ecosystems.

Since 1957

Conservation, Research, Interpretation

The Cave Research Foundation (CRF) is dedicated to exploring, studying, and preserving the remarkable cave and karst landscapes of the United States. Founded in 1957 by pioneering explorers in the Mammoth Cave region of Kentucky, CRF has grown into a national organization supporting scientific research, resource management, and education across multiple parks and public lands. Through sustained partnerships with the National Park Service, U.S. Forest Service, and other agencies, CRF teams conduct fieldwork, mapping, biological studies, and environmental monitoring to advance understanding of underground ecosystems.

Our mission extends beyond exploration - we strive to protect fragile cave environments and share their scientific and cultural significance with the public. By combining the spirit of discovery with a commitment to conservation and interpretation, the Cave Research Foundation continues to illuminate the hidden world beneath us and safeguard it for future generations.

Education and Outreach

The Cave Research Foundation promotes awareness of cave science, conservation, and safety through engaging educational programs. Partnering with the CaveSim Project, CRF brings an interactive, mobile cave experience to schools and community events nationwide. Students explore a realistic simulated cave, gaining a deeper appreciation for underground environments and the importance of protecting these fragile natural systems.
